01 — Studio Identity

Who We Are

GECO Games LTD is an independent game development studio headquartered in Lusaka, Zambia — and one of the most consistently active studios on the African continent.

Co-founded ten years ago by Luyando Shilukukwa and Bulemu Shilukukwa, GECO has grown from a two-person development operation into a multi-vertical ecosystem. We have published over 15 titles across mobile, PC, and web platforms, reached more than 10,000 players across Africa and internationally, and built proprietary infrastructure for African game developers in the form of our Jam Pass payment platform.

We are not a startup discovering games. We are a decade-old studio that has survived, iterated, and built — without external investment — and is now ready to scale.

10 Years in Operation

3 Ecosystem Pillars

GECO operates across four integrated verticals. Game development is our core — original African IP games built for Android-first mobile publishing. Our digital services agency generates ongoing revenue through corporate document design and brand production. The Jam Pass platform constitutes our payment and esports infrastructure. And the GECO Academy is our internal talent development pipeline — training Zambian youth as professional game developers, artists, and narrative designers.

Mission

Make game development accessible and profitable for all African stakeholders — while building original IP that represents African stories authentically in global interactive media.

We have a direct audience reach built over a decade. We have regulatory compliance across PACRA, ZRA, NAPSA, and ZPPA. We have a live, functioning payment platform. And we have the flagship IP — Wacky Ancestors: Alkebulan Game Universe — production-ready and waiting for the partnership BRIDGE was designed to create.

02 — The Project

Wacky Ancestors:
Alkebulan Game Universe

Africa's first major original game IP — a mobile-first action RPG rooted in the real mythologies, cultures, and geographies of the African continent.

The world is called Alkebulan — the ancient name for Africa, reclaimed as the game's universe title. The player is the land's sleeping consciousness, awakening region by region to defeat corrupted forces called the Heralds of the Oorwinnaar and restore the Living Cradle — the spiritual network of the continent — to balance.

Every hero, villain, and environment is drawn from real African cultural tradition. Not invented fantasy with an African skin — reimagined heritage. The North Africa chapter alone draws on Amazigh, Tuareg, Kabyle, and Laguatan mythology, with environmental design sourced from real Saharan geographical data.

GECO is not a single game. It is an integrated ecosystem in which every element reinforces every other — a structure that makes Wacky Ancestors commercially resilient from launch.

01 Original IP
Wacky Ancestors — Alkebulan Game Universe

A full-scale action RPG rooted in African mythology. The game generates IP, player data, and direct revenue — and proves that African storytelling competes globally.

02 Payment Infrastructure
Jam Pass — African Developer Platform

Payment processing and revenue sharing built for African game developers. Enables micro-transactions from ZMW 5 (€0.23). Wacky Ancestors is its flagship proof-of-concept.

03 Talent Pipeline
GECO Academy — Internal Training Programme

4-month production-embedded cohorts training Zambian youth as developers, artists, and narrative designers. Academy trainees actively contribute to Wacky Ancestors production.

Why This Matters for BRIDGE

Wacky Ancestors is not a standalone game pitch. It is the commercial centrepiece of a self-reinforcing ecosystem. The Academy produces the talent that builds the game. The game validates the Jam Pass platform. The platform generates the revenue that funds the next Academy cohort and the next game. A BRIDGE partnership accelerates all three simultaneously.

Jam Pass — Live Infrastructure

Jam Pass is operational. The platform has a working developer portal, player finance dashboard, store management system, and live transaction flow — evidenced by the infrastructure screenshots included in the supporting documents. It has processed approximately 20 test games through the Safari 2048 Showdown and Kopala Survivor esports frameworks, with one title achieving meaningful Zambia-market traction.

  • Revenue model: 1% platform commission on all developer transactions, with 97.5% net settlement to sub-merchants
  • Transaction floor: ZMW 5 (€0.23) — purpose-built for African mobile economies
  • Critical dependency: Swish Master Aggregator MOU — the single unlock for full platform commercial operation
  • Post-BRIDGE target: ZMW 500,000 monthly GTV generating ZMW 5,000/month recurring platform commission
